Scalable Visual Language Robotics (SVLR):
a modular framework for controlling robots using visual and language inputs, based on multi-model approach.

Abstract

The integration of language instructions with robotic control, specifically through Vision Language Action (VLA) models, has shown remarkable potential. However, existing systems are often hampered by high computational demands and the necessity for extensive retraining, limiting their accessibility and scalability.
In this paper, we introduce a scalable, training-free, open-source modular approach named SVLR: Scalable Visual Language Robotics, that operates on consumer-grade GPUs. Our approach integrates a combination of open-source AI models, including the Vision-Language Model Mini-InternVL, zero-shot image segmentation model CLIPSeg, Large Language Model Phi-3, and sentence similarity model all-MiniLM. These models are employed to identify objects in unknown environments, use these objects as parameters for task execution, and generate a sequence of actions in response to language instructions, based on the robot capabilities and a set of pre-programmed robotic tasks.
The scalability of the SVLR framework is demonstrated by its ability to integrate new robotic tasks and robots through the simple addition of text descriptions and programmed tasks to the existing set, as well as by seamlessly incorporating new AI models. This ensures continued compatibility with rapid AI technological advancements and sustained compatibility with a wide range of robots and tasks. The framework was evaluated using a UR10 robotic arm, successfully executing various pick-and-place tasks. Notably, even when deployed on an NVIDIA RTX 2070 (mobile) GPU, our system demonstrated behavior comparable to VLA models, without the need for training.
